### System description and safety notes

**SpreadY** is a multifunctional system consisting of 5 glowing columns. They are intended for the visual identification of sound spreading, as a memory game, that is, for learning addition, and for the analysis and synthesis of words and sentences by turning on individual columns. The selection of functions and detailed settings of the device are made via the VoiceToys mobile application.

### Putting the system into operation

The **SpreadY** columns are used by arranging them in the space so that the **blue** column is closest to the sound source. The other columns are lined up in the following order: **green, yellow, red, gray**. The columns are put into operation by setting the switch on the underside of the device to position 1.

After turning on, the logo on the housing begins to glow. The devices connect automatically, without any additional setup, and react to sound immediately after turning on. The blue column is the main one, the microphone is in it, it sends the signal to the others and it connects to the mobile application.

It is necessary that the **blue** column is active so that the others can function and so that the application connects!

If the logo changes color to yellow, it means that the battery has weakened, and if it starts to flash red, the battery is at a critically low level and should be charged immediately.

You can also monitor the battery status using the mobile application. On the underside of the blue device there are also three openings for the entry of sound into the interior of the **device.** Do not cover them and do not place the device on a soft surface that can absorb sound!

### Charging the device battery

The device contains a lithium-ion battery that is charged with a **voltage of 5V/3A, using the USB-C connector on the underside of the device**. After connecting the voltage, the logo indicates charging by emitting red, yellow or green color, in accordance with the battery status. When it glows constantly green, the charging process is complete.

### Mobile application functions for the SpreadY device

Selecting the operating mode and fine-tuning the device are done using the VoiceToys application. Before launching the mobile application, be sure to turn on the **blue** column, because it contains the bluetooth receiver through which the device communicates with the phone. The devices connect automatically, without any additional setup, and react to sound immediately after turning on.

After turning on the device, on the application's home screen select the option **SpreadY** when its symbol turns green. If the connection is successful, the logo of the blue column will blink twice, and the mobile application will take on the device's settings and display the values according to which it is currently reacting.

In the upper blue field of the application screen you can see the device name and the buttons for system information and help.

**By pressing the gear symbol** in the upper blue field you will access the screen with device information, where you can see whether your device is updated, that is, whether the latest software version has been loaded into it.

By pressing any part of the screen shaded gray you return to the screen for the **SpreadY** device.

## **Microphone** mode


After entering the menu for selecting the device's operating mode by pressing the "MODE" button, you select the mode of visual identification of the sound level by pressing the "Microphone" button. 

<FigureBlock
  src="/voice-toys/images/screenshots/sr/SpreadY_zvuk.jpg"
  alt="SpreadY mikrofon"
  imagePosition="left"
>
*Microphone mode - home screen: battery indicators, preset options, sliders, display selection and system information.*


The visual representation of sound can be analog or digital. The buttons for selecting the visual representation of sound, "Analog" and "Digital", are located in the middle of the application screen. In analog mode, all the columns glow together, and the intensity of the glow depends on the sound level. In digital mode the columns always glow at maximum intensity when the sound exceeds the level defined for each of them, and they light up one by one, depending on the sound level.

The adjustment of the sound level, sensitivity and decay in the analog mode, that is, the sound level, sensitivity and light intensity in the digital mode of visual representation of sound, is done using the sliders located in the lower half of the application screen, in the **"Settings"** section.

Sound level is the threshold that the sound level must cross in order to cause a light reaction. Sounds below this level are ignored. If you need to, adjust it according to the noise level in your space so that the **blue** column does not turn on when no one is speaking.

Sensitivity is the system's sensitivity to a change in sound level.

If you change it toward higher values, the system becomes more sensitive, and it can be triggered more easily. By moving the slider toward lower values, a higher sound level is needed for the device to be triggered, that is, for the lights to turn on.

Decay determines the decay of the light intensity from the blue to the gray column.

**Light intensity** adjusts the brightness of the light columns. The more you move it toward higher values, the brighter the light columns will glow.

If you change the parameters of sound level, sensitivity, light intensity or decay, **you can save your settings** in the way described on the following page.

## Memory mode - settings

**3-7:** After entering the menu for selecting the device's operating mode by pressing the **"MODE"** button, you select the mode of the memory game and learning addition by pressing the **"Memory"** button. The screen shown on the previous page will appear, in whose upper blue field you can see the mode the device is in and the buttons for system information and help. The function and the way of using these buttons are described on page 15.

The memory game generates sequences of random numbers from 1 to 5 and displays them by lighting up the columns. By adjusting the parameters, which is done using the application, you can determine the level of complexity of the game. The adjustment of the parameters is done using the sliders located in the lower part of the screen, whose layout is shown on the previous page.

<FigureBlock
  src="/voice-toys/images/screenshots/sr/SpreadY_memorija_1.jpg"
  alt="zupcanik VibeY"
  imagePosition="right"
>
*Memory mode - game parameters screen: series, exposure, pause, number of columns, intensity and commands.*

**Number of series** determines how many series of column lighting there will be during one game. The smallest number of series is 1, and the largest is 10.

**Maximum size** indicates how many columns we will use during one series. We can determine that only one lights up, all the way up to all five columns. Once set, this parameter remains the same in all the series.

**Display duration** indicates the time during which the columns will be lit. The time is expressed in seconds.

**Pause duration** indicates the time during which the columns will be off between two series. The time is expressed in seconds.

**Light intensity** determines the intensity of the light with which the columns will be lit.

If you change the parameters of sound level, sensitivity, light intensity or decay, you can save your settings in the way described on page 19.

Below the sliders for adjusting the game parameters there are the PLAY ▶️ , REPEAT 🔁  and ARROW 🔽 buttons. Their purpose is explained on the following page.

## Memory mode - principle of operation

<FigureBlock
  src="/voice-toys/images/screenshots/sr/SpreadY_memorija_2-2.jpg"
  alt="SpreadY memorija"
  imagePosition="right"
>
You start the game by pressing the ▶️ button. The columns will begin to light up, and on the screen the scheme according to which they lit up will be displayed. The view of this screen is shown in the image on the right.

The numbers in the left column indicate the number of the column-lighting series. In this example the game was limited to 5 series. The numbers in the right column indicate how many columns were lit in that series. In this example, in the first series 3 columns were lit, in the second also 3, in the third 2, and in the fourth and fifth one each.

The numbers in the bottom row indicate how many columns of a particular color were lit in total during the game. In this example there were 2 **blue** in total, 3 green, 1 yellow, 2 each of red and gray. The number 10 in the right corner indicates how many columns in total were lit during the game.

The colored fields indicate the columns of a particular color that lit up during each of the series. In this example, in the first series the green, yellow and gray columns were lit, in the second the **blue**, red and gray, and so on in turn.

The application determines by random selection which columns will light up in which series, and how many columns will light up in which series in relation to the set maximum display size, which is adjusted with the parameter described on the previous page.

By pressing the ▶️ button you start a new game with the same parameters that were previously set.

By pressing the 🔁 button you repeat the previous game, with the same set parameters.

If you want to change the parameters, by pressing the 🔽 button the sliders for controlling the game parameters will appear. You can remove them by pressing the same button again.
</FigureBlock>

### **Manual** mode


<FigureBlock
  src="/voice-toys/images/screenshots/sr/SpreadY_ruc_no.jpg"
  alt="zupcanik VibeY"
  imagePosition="right"
>
***Manual** mode: battery indicators, intensity, column activation and mode controls.*

After entering the menu for selecting the device's operating mode by pressing the **"MODE"** button, you select the mode of the memory game and learning addition by pressing the **"Manual"** button. The screen shown on the previous page will appear, in whose upper blue field you can see the mode the device is in and the buttons for system information and help. The function and the way of using these buttons are described on page 15.

We note that in this case too, the same arrangement of columns applies as for the "Microphone" mode - **blue**, **green, yellow, red, gray**, as is also shown on the application screen.

For the analysis and synthesis of words and sentences, accessories are used - holders that are placed on the glowing rods, into which you can put cards with letters or images. The cards are not part of the **"VoiceToys"** system and do not come with the SpreadY columns.

The adjustment of the glow intensity is done with the "Light intensity" slider. It applies to all the columns.

The determination of the way of activating and displaying the lit columns is set by selecting one of the options shown in the following section.

**One** indicates the option in which only one column will light up and remain lit. We can light a column by pressing the field that is bordered with the color of the column we want to light. A colored field indicates that the column of that color is lit. We can choose which column we want to light, and when we light the next column - the previous one will turn off. We can light the columns in order from blue to gray and back by pressing the LEFT ⬅️ and RIGHT ➡️ buttons, and we turn off the columns by pressing the ↻ button.

**Any** indicates the option in which, by pressing the field that is bordered with the color of the column we want to light, we light one or more columns. We turn off a column by pressing the colored field again.

**Random** indicates the option in which the application will light one column by a random sample. The column will light up after pressing the PLAY ▶️ button, and we will turn it off and be ready for the next lighting by pressing the ↻ button. A colored field indicates which column is lit.
</FigureBlock>
